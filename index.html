Truth or Myth: Test your knowledge and flip to reveal the truth

AI can combine reasoning with action to solve complex problems.

Truth

With agentic architecture, AI can both “think” through information and take action on it. This means it can analyze data, make informed choices and carry out tasks—helping teams solve challenges faster and more reliably.

Agents are becoming more proactive rather than reactive.

Truth

AI agents are evolving from waiting for commands to anticipating needs. They can now plan tasks in advance, work behind the scenes and assist continuously—so work gets done more seamlessly with less oversight.

AI can completely replace human expertise.

Myth

AI is powerful, but it can't replace human judgment, creativity or empathy. Leaders are still essential for strategy, ethical decisions and understanding people—AI works best as a partner that extends human expertise.

Truth or Myth: Test your knowledge and flip to reveal the truth

Generative AI can produce entirely new insights from raw data.

Myth

Generative AI doesn't invent from scratch. It analyzes patterns in its training data and reassembles them to surface insights—it's synthesis, not pure creation.

Runtime inferences are becoming more critical than training large models.

Truth

The real impact of AI comes from how well models perform in production. Runtime inference—fast, accurate and reliable outputs—drives business value more than model size.

Small language models are insufficient for real-world financial applications.

Myth

Smaller models are often better suited for real-time financial applications. Their agility, efficiency & lower cost make them ideal where speed, compliance, & precision are critical.
